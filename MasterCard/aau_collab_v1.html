Cover Story · Collaboration

Five Institutions, One Continental Vision for Climate Action

The Africa Climate Collaborative Convening 2026 brings together the Association of African Universities, University of Cambridge, University of Cape Town, University of Ghana, Makerere University, and the Mastercard Foundation to design a unified research, policy and capacity-building roadmap for a climate-resilient Africa.

Across five days in Accra, more than 240 researchers, vice-chancellors, policymakers and youth delegates will co-author Africa's largest interdisciplinary climate platform to date. The convening is anchored on the belief that no single institution, country or sector can deliver the continent's climate transition alone — only sustained, structured collaboration can.

Working groups will tackle adaptive agriculture, urban resilience, just-energy transitions, climate finance, and the integration of indigenous knowledge into national climate strategies. Outcomes will feed directly into the AU Climate Strategy mid-term review and into the partner universities' joint PhD pipeline funded by the Mastercard Foundation.

“Climate change does not respect borders or disciplines. Our response must be equally borderless — rooted in African scholarship and powered by global partnership.”

— Prof. Olusola Oyewole, Secretary-General, AAU

Partner Spotlight

What each partner brings to the table

Cambridge Centre for Climate Repair

Leading the modelling and earth-systems research workstream, with three new joint postdoctoral fellowships open to African scholars.

UCT African Climate & Development Initiative

Anchors the just-transitions track and convenes the youth delegates programme drawing students from 18 African countries.

Institute for Environment & Sanitation Studies

Host institution for the convening and lead on West African coastal resilience and food-systems research.

Makerere Centre for Climate Change Research

Coordinates the East African node and leads adaptation research focused on smallholder agriculture and water security.

Lead Funding Partner

Mastercard Foundation backs $14M continental climate fellowship

A new five-year programme will fund 200 African climate researchers across the partner universities, with priority for young scholars and women in STEM.
