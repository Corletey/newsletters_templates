Lead Story · Africa Climate Collaborative Convening 2026

Six partners. One agenda. A continental answer to the climate decade.

By the AAU Editorial Desk · Accra

Above: Mathew Ntaganira (Mastercard Foundation) joins partner institutions to launch the convening series.

For the first time in the Association's seventy-year history, six institutions spanning three continents will sit in single working session to co-author a binding climate research and policy roadmap for Africa.

The Africa Climate Collaborative — convened by the AAU and the Mastercard Foundation, with the universities of Cambridge, Cape Town, Ghana, and Makerere as founding research anchors — arrives at a moment when the continent shoulders the heaviest climate impacts while emitting the least.

Over five days in Accra, delegates will operationalise four shared deliverables: a continental climate data atlas, a 200-fellow doctoral pipeline, a joint policy brief to the African Union, and an open-access curriculum on climate justice to be adopted by every AAU member university by 2028.

"This is not a conference," noted the AAU Secretary-General in pre-convening remarks. "It is the formal moment at which African higher education stops responding to the climate crisis and starts authoring the response."
