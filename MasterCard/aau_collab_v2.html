AAU × Africa Climate Collaborative
 
 

◆ Headline Story ◆

Africa's universities unite for the continent's boldest climate compact

From Cape Town to Kampala, four leading African universities have joined Cambridge and the Mastercard Foundation to launch a continent-wide climate research and policy coalition — with its inaugural convening hosted in Accra this July.

 

Article 01 · Co-Investment

Mastercard Foundation commits $14M to climate scholarship pipeline

The five-year programme — the largest single private investment ever channelled through the AAU — will support 200 doctoral and post-doctoral African climate scholars across Cambridge, Cape Town, Ghana and Makerere. At least 60% of the fellowships are reserved for women researchers, and a further 20% for scholars from fragile and conflict-affected states.

Fellows will be embedded in cross-institutional research clusters — a deliberate move away from siloed grant-making and toward sustained collaborative authorship of African climate science.

 

Article 02 · Joint Research

A shared atlas: mapping climate risk across nine African ecosystems

The Pan-African Climate Risk Atlas — co-developed by Cape Town's ACDI, Makerere's CCCR and Cambridge's Centre for Climate Repair — will publish its first open-data release at the convening. The atlas integrates satellite observation, hydrological modelling and community-collected indigenous indicators across nine ecological zones.

Ministries of environment in twelve countries have already requested early access to the dataset to inform their updated Nationally Determined Contributions.

Article 03 · Voices

Youth at the centre: 60 student delegates to co-author the Accra Declaration

For the first time, a continental climate declaration will be drafted with binding youth representation. Sixty undergraduate and graduate delegates — selected through open competition from all five AAU regions — will sit on every working group and co-sign the closing communique.
