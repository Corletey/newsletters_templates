01

The Collaborative, in brief

Cambridge, Ghana, Makerere and Cape Town are strengthening research, data and capabilities for green economies. The AAU serves as backbone organisation and CoCreative as technical partner — all working toward 30 million young people in dignified work by 2030.

02

Accra Workshop wrap-up

Partners met 22–25 April to map shared purpose and stand up a Stewardship Team. The AAU led logistics and documentation, and is drafting the Theory of Change and the MEL framework.

03

Partner progress at a glance

Cambridge 16 Master’s + 6 PhD scholars hosted; Africa Reference Group established.
Ghana Fellowship & Catalyst Grant calls live; 4 research-lab site visits done.
Makerere Workplan reviewed; 500-youth green-skilling programme in preparation.
Cape Town Proposal in final review; Doctoral Training Academy planned.

Looking ahead: the 2026 Convening

With work plans moving into implementation (2026–2032), partners are preparing for the next Africa Climate Collaborative Convening — an annual moment to share research and grow the continent’s climate “network of networks.”
