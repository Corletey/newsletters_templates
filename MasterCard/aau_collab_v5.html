The Climate Resilience & Sustainability Collaborative unites four universities, the AAU and the Mastercard Foundation around research, talent and green jobs.

In this issue

Four universities. One shared vision for a green economy.

The University of Cambridge, University of Ghana, Makerere University and the University of Cape Town are strengthening research, data and capabilities for green economies — with the Association of African Universities as backbone organisation and CoCreative as technical partner.

The shared goal: help 30 million young people — especially young women — secure dignified, fulfilling work by 2030.

The work

Four streams driving change

01  Workforce Development

Degrees, diplomas and leadership skills that move young people into green jobs and innovation.

02  Entrepreneurship

Generating, accelerating and scaling climate-smart ventures, with a path to commercialisation.

03  Research & Innovation

Regional centres of excellence delivering interdisciplinary research and teaching.

04  Networks, Policy & Learning

A pan-African “network of networks” transforming the climate policy agenda.

Field report

Accra Workshop, 22–25 April

Partners mapped the Collaborative’s purpose and critical shifts, and stood up a Stewardship Team with a representative from each university. The AAU led logistics and documentation, and is drafting the Theory of Change and the Monitoring, Evaluation, Learning & Adaptation framework.

From the partners

Where each university stands

University of Cambridge

First cohort of 16 Master’s and 6 PhD scholars hosted; Africa Reference Group for climate ventures established; green-jobs workshops held in Cape Town.

University of Ghana

Fellowship and Catalyst Grant calls live; four site visits completed for Climate Resilience Research Labs; gap-analysis validation workshop held with 53 stakeholders.

Makerere University

Proposal approved and workplan reviewed; preparing a green-entrepreneurship programme for 500 youth (70% women) and a Climate Resilience Support Centre.

University of Cape Town

Revised proposal in final review; planning a Doctoral Training Academy, an integrated Master’s programme and community-led climate innovation.

“Leveraging the power and influence of higher education institutions to drive climate and sustainability impact — by centering youth in Africa.”

Implementation runs 2026–2032, with an annual convening to share progress.
