◆  Lead Story

Four universities, one green vision
[ PHOTO — partners at the Accra workshop, 1240×620 ]

The Climate Resilience & Sustainability Collaborative brings together the University of Cambridge, University of Ghana, Makerere University and the University of Cape Town to strengthen research, data and capabilities for green economies across Africa — driving evidence-based decision-making, innovation and dignified green jobs for young people.

Supported by the Mastercard Foundation, the Collaborative is anchored by the Association of African Universities as backbone organisation, with CoCreative as technical partner. Together the network is working toward the Foundation’s goal of enabling 30 million young people — particularly young women — to secure dignified and fulfilling work by 2030.

◆  Feature

Accra hosts the Climate Collaborative Workshop

From 22–25 April, partners gathered in Accra to map the Collaborative’s shared purpose, critical shifts and systems-change thinking, and to stand up a Stewardship Team with a representative from each university.

The AAU was contracted to lead logistics and post-conference documentation — and to develop a draft Theory of Change and a Monitoring, Evaluation, Learning & Adaptation framework.

◆  Partner Snapshots

University of Cambridge

First cohort of 16 Master’s and 6 PhD scholars hosted; the Africa Reference Group for climate ventures is established.

University of Ghana

Fellowship and Catalyst Grant calls are live; four site visits completed for new Climate Resilience Research Labs.

Makerere University

Proposal approved; a green-entrepreneurship programme will skill 500 youth—70% women—and launch a Climate Resilience Support Centre.

University of Cape Town

A Doctoral Training Academy and integrated Master’s programme anchor work to build employability and community-led climate innovation.

◆  Looking ahead

Toward the 2026 Convening

As work plans move into implementation (2026–2032), partners are preparing for the next Africa Climate Collaborative Convening — an annual moment to share research, monitor progress and grow the continent’s “network of networks” in climate resilience and sustainability.
