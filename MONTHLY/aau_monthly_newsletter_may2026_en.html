AAU Leads CESA’s Higher Education, Research & Innovation Cluster (2026–2035)

The AAU has been designated as the lead institution for the Higher Education, Research and Innovation (HERI) Cluster under the African Union’s Continental Education Strategy for Africa (CESA 2026–2035). AAU will provide strategic leadership and collaborate with AU member states, universities, and development partners to strengthen quality assurance, research capacity, digital transformation, and equitable access across the continent. With over 450 member universities and prior experience coordinating CESA 2016–2025, AAU is well-positioned to advance the quality, relevance, and global competitiveness of African higher education systems.

AAU to Host Global University Associations Forum 2026

The AAU will host the Global University Associations Forum 2026 (GUAF) in Accra, Ghana, in November 2026, under the theme “Knowledge Without Borders: Collaboration in a Fragmented World.” Established in 2021, GUAF brings together leading university associations from Africa, Europe, Asia, the Americas, and the Arab region to foster dialogue and joint action on global issues. As host, AAU will strengthen equitable knowledge partnerships, expand digital connectivity, promote academic mobility, and amplify African perspectives in global higher education discourse.

AAU Governing Board Meets in Accra to Advance Strategic Priorities

The AAU Governing Board convened its 86th session on 12–13 February 2026 at the AAU Secretariat in Accra, Ghana, bringing together vice-chancellors and higher education leaders from across Africa. Key discussions focused on advancing quality assurance, research excellence, digital transformation, and continental collaboration. The session also covered preparations for COREVIP 2027, institutional sustainability, and concluded with the launch of the QS World Rankings: Sub-Saharan Africa 2026.

AAU and KCA University Partner to Host COREVIP 2027 and AAU@60 Celebration

The AAU and KCA University have signed a Memorandum of Understanding to co-host the 24th Conference of Rectors, Vice-Chancellors and Presidents of African Universities (COREVIP 2027) in Nairobi, Kenya, from 20–23 July 2027. The event coincides with the AAU’s 60th anniversary and will convene under the theme “Repositioning African Universities for Resilience, Relevance, and Impact.” COREVIP 2027 will bring together university leaders, policymakers, researchers, and development partners from across Africa and beyond to deliberate on the future of higher education on the continent.

AAU Digital Empowerment MOOCs: Scaling Capacity Building for African Higher Education

The AAU, in partnership with eLearnAfrica, launched the Digital Empowerment Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) to equip academic and administrative staff across Africa with critical digital competencies. Co-created with African ICT experts, the courses focus on cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, and foundational digital skills tailored to the realities of African universities. The initiative has grown into a continent-wide learning community, with the second cohort currently underway and an in-person Digital Leadership Workshop planned for July 2026 in Accra.

AAU, Taylor & Francis Webinar Series on Medical Research

A three-part webinar series on advancing medical research and publishing, organised by the AAU in partnership with Taylor & Francis, brought together scholars, practitioners, and experts to strengthen knowledge-sharing in healthcare research. Sessions covered the role of preprints in medicine (4 February), ethical publishing practices and data sharing (24 February), and the use of AI in medical research (16 March 2026), recording a combined total of over 540 registrations. The series successfully built capacity, promoted best practices, and fostered collaboration across the medical research ecosystem.

Fix the system, not the women: African universities urged to dismantle hurdles to female leadership

At an AAU-hosted International Women’s Day 2026 webinar, experts urged African universities to reform institutional systems that limit women’s rise to leadership, rather than merely expanding access. Speakers argued that structural barriers — including biased recruitment, weak mentorship, and hostile work environments — continue to prevent women from fully exercising leadership and authority despite improved enrolment. The webinar concluded that advancing women into leadership is a strategic imperative for Africa’s development, and the AAU reaffirmed its commitment through leadership capacity-building, mentorship, and strategic partnerships.

Positioning African Universities for Global Visibility: AAU Strengthens Capacity Across the Continent

The AAU launched the “Positioning Your University for Global Visibility” workshops to equip African universities with practical strategies to strengthen their global presence, research impact, and institutional reputation. Two successful editions have been held — in Accra (May 2025) with 37 participants from 9 countries, and in Lusaka (January 2026) with 61 participants from 30 institutions across 15 countries. A third edition is planned in Kenya from 14–17 July 2026, focusing on digital marketing, AI and digital transformation, and university–industry innovation ecosystems.

Cooperation at Technical Level Lays Ground for Comparability of Quality Assurance Systems in Africa under HAQAA3

Under the third phase of the Harmonisation of African Higher Education Quality Assurance and Accreditation (HAQAA3) initiative, the AAU and ENQA are collaborating to strengthen external reviews of African quality assurance agencies against the African Standards and Guidelines for Quality Assurance (ASG-QA). The AAU-hosted interim technical unit in Accra is playing a central coordinating role, with site visits already completed in Lesotho and Namibia in early 2026. These efforts lay the foundation for the Pan-African Quality Assurance and Accreditation Alliance (PAQAA), which will host a continental register of compliant quality assurance agencies.

Strengthening AU–EU Collaboration for Inclusive Entrepreneurship: How AEIP and AAU Are Shaping the Future of Innovation

The AAU is playing a central leadership role in the Africa–Europe Innovation Platform (AEIP), a key instrument of the AU–EU Innovation Agenda (2023–2033), connecting innovators, researchers, and policymakers to co-create solutions in entrepreneurship, climate action, and capacity building. In 2026, the AAU launched three thematic Communities of Practice — on entrepreneurship and innovation, commercialisation, and climate change and sustainability education — two of which have already produced policy briefs influencing AU–EU engagements. Supported by dedicated LinkedIn groups and interactive digital platforms, these Communities of Practice are turning dialogue into policy-relevant knowledge and practical impact.

Strengthening Institutional Readiness for Climate Adaptation in Africa: The CLARE CO-CAT Approach

Under the CLARE project, twenty African universities participated in Virtual Living Lab workshops (October 2025–March 2026) to reflect on institutional capacity for climate adaptation research. Findings revealed that barriers are largely systemic — including weak interdisciplinary collaboration, poor policy engagement, and research incentives that prioritise publications over real-world impact. The CO-CAT framework was developed to assess institutional readiness holistically across ten domains including governance, leadership, partnerships, inclusion, and financial sustainability.

Bridging the Gap: New AAU-Led Research Targets Inequities in Sub-Saharan African Higher Education

The AAU, with support from the British Council, is leading a major research initiative on access to higher education in Sub-Saharan Africa, covering seven countries: Rwanda, Ethiopia, Malawi, South Africa, Nigeria, Kenya, and Ghana. The study employs a mixed-methods approach and is developing two innovative tools — a Digital Readiness Scorecard and an Equity and Inclusion Index — to assess institutional capacity and inclusiveness. Final outputs, including country and cross-country reports and a policy options paper, are expected in May 2026 and will inform decision-making to promote equitable access across the region.
