From the Executive Secretary

Charting the Future of African Higher Education

Dear colleagues, this April edition arrives at an exciting inflection point for the continent. With new cross-border research agreements, expanded mobility programmes, and a renewed commitment from heads of state, African universities are poised for transformative growth. Inside, you will find highlights from our recent policy convenings, emerging research breakthroughs, and a look ahead at what May holds for our community of over 400 member institutions across 46 countries.

◆ Featured Stories

Research

Pan-African AI Consortium Secures $2.4M in Seed Funding

Twelve AAU member universities across Nigeria, Kenya, South Africa, Ghana, Senegal, and Egypt have launched a joint artificial intelligence research consortium. The initiative will focus on responsible AI governance, localised language models, and climate-resilient agricultural technologies tailored to African contexts.

Policy

AAU Presents Landmark Paper at AU Summit on Education Financing

The Executive Secretary addressed the African Union Summit with a position paper urging member states to allocate a minimum of 1.5% of GDP to higher education research infrastructure by 2030. The paper received endorsement from 22 heads of state and paves the way for a continental compact on education financing.

Cover Story

The Rise of Intra-African Student Mobility

A new AAU report reveals that intra-African student mobility has grown 34% since 2022. Driven by harmonised credit-transfer frameworks and the African Continental Free Trade Area, students are increasingly choosing to study at peer institutions in neighbouring countries. The report profiles mobility corridors between East, West, and Southern Africa and makes recommendations for scaling exchange programmes.
