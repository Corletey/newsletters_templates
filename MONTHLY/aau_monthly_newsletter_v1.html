Cover Story

Reimagining Higher Education for Africa's Next Generation

As Africa's youth population continues its rapid growth — projected to double by 2050 — the continent's universities face an unprecedented challenge and opportunity. The Association of African Universities has convened a high-level task force to chart a bold new framework for continental higher education, one that prioritises digital access, vocational integration, and locally anchored research agendas.

Task force co-chair Prof. Amina Diallo of Université Cheikh Anta Diop described the initiative as "a once-in-a-generation chance to decolonise the curriculum and ensure that African universities serve African futures." The framework is expected to be presented at the next AAU General Conference in Kigali.

Regional Roundup

West Africa

ECOWAS University Twinning Programme Expands to 30 Institutions

Building on early successes in Ghana and Senegal, the ECOWAS twinning programme now pairs 30 universities to share faculty, curricula, and research infrastructure across the sub-region. The expansion was facilitated through AAU's regional coordination office in Abuja.

East Africa

EAC Launches Joint PhD Programme in Agricultural Technology

Seven universities spanning Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda, and Rwanda have jointly launched a PhD programme focused on precision agriculture and food systems innovation. The inaugural cohort of 120 doctoral students will begin in September 2026.

North Africa

Cairo Declaration Commits Five Nations to Mutual Credit Recognition

Education ministers from Egypt, Morocco, Tunisia, Algeria, and Libya signed the Cairo Declaration, agreeing to mutually recognise academic credits and degrees across their national systems — a landmark step for student mobility in the Maghreb and Nile regions.
