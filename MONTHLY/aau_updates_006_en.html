Pillar 1: Digital Transformation & Online Learning

1

Academic Leadership Summit (ALS) 2026

The AAU invites faculty members, instructional designers, IT professionals, and academic leaders from across the continent to participate in the Academic Leadership Summit (ALS) 2026, to be held under the theme, “Designing, Developing, and Delivering High-Impact Online Learning in African Universities.” The five-day summit is designed to strengthen the capacity of African higher education institutions to create engaging, scalable, and contextually relevant online learning experiences. Adopting a unique “Build-As-You-Learn” approach, the programme will provide participants with practical, hands-on experience in online course design, digital content development, assessment strategies, learning management systems, and learner support. Participants will work directly on their own institutional courses and materials and will leave the summit with fully developed online courses and implementation plans that can be adapted and scaled within their institutions. Through this initiative, AAU seeks to accelerate digital transformation and foster innovation in teaching and learning across Africa’s higher education landscape. The Summit also offers a valuable platform for networking and knowledge exchange with peers and experts from across the continent.

Participation in this event requires a registration fee of:

USD 700 – AAU Member Universities

USD 800 – Non-Member Universities

This fee covers facilitation, training materials, and meals. Participants are responsible for their own travel and accommodation expenses. Due to limited slots, early registration is strongly encouraged.

Date: 5–9 October 2026 Deadline: 2 September 2026
Venue: AAU Secretariat, Accra, Ghana
Enquiries: aaulms@aau.org / commserve@aau.org

4

Higher Education Dialogue Webinar Series

The AAU through its Africa Europe Innovation Platform (AEIP) invites higher education leaders, quality assurance practitioners and academics to participate in the inaugural session of the Higher Education Dialogue Webinar Series on the topic: “Building Trust through Qualifications Frameworks and Quality Assurance Systems.”

The session will explore the African Continental Qualifications Framework (ACQF), Pan-African quality assurance and accreditation systems, the HAQAA Initiative, and international experiences in qualifications transparency and recognition. Participants will also discuss how these mechanisms can improve qualification comparability, support recognition decisions, and advance the harmonisation of higher education across Africa.

Date: 28 July 2026 Time: 10:00 GMT Format: Virtual

Pillar 3: Research Capacity & Scholarly Publication

8

AAU Pan-African Interdisciplinary Academic Writing, Research and Publication Mentorship and Support Programme

The Association of African Universities (AAU), through its Early Career Researchers Centre of Excellence (ECRs CoE) and in collaboration with its Southern Africa Regional Office (SARO) and European Regional Office (ERO), invites applications for the AAU Pan-African Interdisciplinary Academic Writing, Research and Publication Mentorship and Support Pilot Programme.

This 12-month online mentorship programme is designed for doctoral candidates, postdoctoral researchers, lecturers, academics, and research-active professionals who have clear publication goals and existing datasets or draft manuscripts. The programme aims to support participants in developing high-quality, publishable research outputs and positioning their work within African and international scholarly debates.

Participation is limited to approximately 35–40 participants to maintain the quality of engagement and mentorship. Interested applicants are encouraged to apply early.

Programme Start Date: August 2026

Programme Fee: USD 750

Payment Link: https://pay.aau.org/early-career-researchers/

Pillar 4: Student Leadership & Youth Engagement

9

African Students’ Leadership Summit 2026

AAU in collaboration with its North Africa Regional Office (NARO), hosted by Al-Azhar University, invites participation in the inaugural African Students’ Leadership Summit 2026, to be held in Cairo, Egypt. Convened under the theme “Shaping the Next Generation of African Transformation: Leadership, Innovation, and Agenda 2063 in Action,” the Summit is a high-level continental platform designed to equip emerging student leaders with the leadership capacity, strategic thinking skills, and innovation mindset needed to contribute meaningfully to Africa’s development. Bringing together undergraduate and graduate students, doctoral researchers, and young innovators from across the continent, the Summit aligns with Agenda 2063 and the Continental Education Strategy for Africa (CESA) by promoting youth empowerment and active participation in shaping Africa’s future. Participants will engage in dynamic sessions, foster cross-continental networks, and receive certificates of participation in recognition of their engagement. Institutions are strongly encouraged to sponsor diverse student delegations as part of strengthening Africa’s future leadership pipeline.

Participation Fees:

USD 300 – AAU Member Institutions

USD 350 – Non-AAU Member Institutions
